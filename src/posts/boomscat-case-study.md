# Dig In: What Happened When Boomscat Got Their Questions Early

Boomscat hadn't performed in years. When they finally booked their return show, the plan included a talk back at the end of the night. A chance for the audience to hear directly from the artists about why now, why this, why again.

That is where Mouse &amp; Matter came in.

## The Problem With Most Talk Backs

Most artist Q&As fall into the same trap. The host asks surface questions. The artist gives surface answers. The audience claps politely and goes home having learned nothing they could not have guessed.

The issue is not the artist. It is the prep. Nobody hands an artist the real questions ahead of time. They walk into the room cold, get asked something they have never actually sat with, and reach for the safest answer available. Not because they are hiding something. Because nobody gave them the room to think first.

## What We Built Instead

Before the show, Mouse &amp; Matter built a primer. Six questions designed to get Boomscat thinking days before the event, not seconds before the mic turned on. Questions like what does this project mean to you now versus when you started, and what do you need this return to do for you personally, not for the fans, not for the music.

One question was held back entirely and saved for the room. The kind of question where the pause before the answer says as much as the answer itself.

The primer was not the interview. It was the warm up. The goal was simple: get the real thinking done in advance so that when the audience was finally in the room, the depth was already there.

## What Happened in the Room

The talk back became something closer to an intimate conversation than a press junket. The audience got to know Boomscat in a way that felt honest, not rehearsed. According to the band, there were questions in that room they had genuinely never sat with before the primer arrived in their inbox. Questions about why now, what changed, and what this chapter actually means to them.

That is the result you get when preparation replaces performance.

Afterward, Boomscat described feeling filled and ready to create more.

That is not a metric. That is the entire point.

## The Takeaway

This was not a marketing campaign. It was not a funnel. It was one show, one band, one night. But the principle underneath it is the same one Mouse &amp; Matter applies to every product, every brand, every team we work with.

People do not connect with polish. They connect with honesty that had room to surface. The job is not to script that honesty. It is to build the conditions that let it show up.

That is the work. Dig in, find the real thing, and give it room to breathe.
