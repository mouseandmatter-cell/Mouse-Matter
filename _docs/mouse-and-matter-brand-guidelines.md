## The Methodology: The Selector

In music, the Selector is the one who hears what comes next before anyone else does. That is how Mouse & Matter works.

**Step 1 — Listen to the Market**
We start where most PMMs skip. Customer success conversations. Sales calls. Win/loss analysis. What people say is broken. What they love. What they wish existed. The signal is always there. We find it and turn it into strategy.

**Step 2 — Read the Room**
We track what competitors are doing, where they show up, how they talk, and where their gaps are. We build and continuously refine personas that reflect who the audience actually is — not just their job title, but their full life and what they need right now.

**Step 3 — Connect with Product**
We sit with product teams regularly — not to report what marketing needs, but to bring them what the market is saying. New launches, feature prioritization, positioning for what is coming. Product and go-to-market should never move in different directions.

**Step 4 — Enable the Team**
All that research becomes something the team can actually use. The messaging framework. The sales materials. The content strategy. The conference presence. We build the system that makes the team capable of executing confidently without us in every room.

## Voice & Tone

Mouse & Matter is an extension of one person. The voice should always feel like that — a senior strategist who is direct, confident, and deeply curious. Not a brand performing confidence. An actual person who has done the work and knows how to talk about it plainly.

### This voice is:

**Direct without being blunt**
We say what we mean. No hedging, no corporate softening, no "leveraging synergies." But we are never harsh. We are honest in the way a trusted colleague is honest.

**Confident without being arrogant**
We do not need to announce that we are good at this. The way we talk about the work demonstrates it. Let the thinking speak.

**Warm but not casual**
This is not a brand that uses exclamation points or calls people "friend." But it is also not cold. There is real care here for the people we work with and the problems we help them solve.

**Specific over general**
We do not say "we help companies grow." We say "we find the words and the structure that make your audience care." Specificity is credibility.

**Unhurried**
The sentences do not rush. We are not trying to impress anyone with volume. One clear idea, fully expressed, is worth more than five bullet points.

### What we avoid:

- Jargon that makes simple things sound complicated
- Buzzwords: "innovative," "disruptive," "best-in-class," "game-changing"
- Passive voice
- Overselling or hype
- Anything that sounds like it came from a template

### Tone by context:

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Website | Confident, unhurried, inviting |
| LinkedIn posts | Direct, thoughtful, point of view-led |
| Client proposals | Clear, specific, warm |
| Case studies | Story-driven, grounded in real outcomes |
| Conference materials | Authoritative but accessible |
| Emails | Human, never templated |

## Color Palette — Amber Authority

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Background | `#0A0908` | Primary dark backgrounds |
| Panel | `#131210` | Cards, sections on dark bg |
| Accent | `#D4952A` | Primary brand color, CTAs, labels, rules |
| Accent Light | `#E8C878` | Headlines on dark backgrounds |
| Signal | `#8AAB72` | Tertiary accent, used sparingly |
| Cream | `#F5F0E8` | Light background alternative |
| Dark Text | `#0A0908` | Body text on light backgrounds |
| Mid Text | `#3a3530` | Body text, secondary |
| Muted | `#9a8c78` | Labels, captions, metadata |

**Usage notes:**
- Amber (#D4952A) is the heartbeat of the brand. It should always be present.
- On light backgrounds: dark text with amber accents
- On dark backgrounds: cream/amber text with amber accents
- Signal green (#8AAB72) is a supporting color only — never the lead
- Never use more than three colors in a single asset

## Typography

| Role | Typeface | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Display / Headlines | DM Serif Display | Hero headlines, section titles, pull quotes |
| Utility / Labels | DM Mono | Labels, tags, metadata, captions, document markers |
| Body | Karla | All body copy, descriptions, paragraphs |

**Type rules:**
- Headlines: DM Serif Display, use italic for emphasis and warmth
- All-caps labels: DM Mono only, letter-spacing 0.15em+, small size (9–11px)
- Body copy: Karla at 15–16px, line-height 1.7–1.8
- Never use more than these three typefaces in a single asset
